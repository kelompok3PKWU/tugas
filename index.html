Solo liwet rice

Nasi liwet is made by ordinary people living in Menuran Village, Baki District, Sukoharjo Regency. Around 1934, the Menuran community began trying to sell nasi liwet to the Solo or Surakarta area. From here, nasi liwet began to be known and consumed by noble families and Kasunanan.

Rp.25.000

Tools And Materials

The tools needed are pan, knife, basket, tray, spatula, ladle. And the ingredients needed are 400 grams of rice, washed clean 700 ml of coconut milk from 1/2 coconut 2 pandan leaves 2 bay leaves 1 stalk of lemongrass, bruised, 1/2 teaspoon of salt

Solo liwet rice recipe

400 grams of rice, washed, 700 ml of coconut milk from 1/2 coconut, 2 pandan leaves, 2 bay leaves, 1 stalk of lemongrass, 1/2 tsp salt, 1 chicken, 1 tsp coriander, 2 tbsp brown sugar, 2 cups of coconut water, 4 cloves of garlic, 6 cloves of shallots, 3 roasted candlenuts, 3 cm of galangal, 1/4 tsp shrimp paste.

How To Make

* Wash the rice, then mix with coconut milk, salt, lemongrass and bay leaves. Cook until done, then set aside

* Puree: bamer baput kemiri then saute until fragrant, add bay leaves and lemongrass. Then add coconut milk and add chicken. Then add salt and stock powder Cook until done, then shred.

* Saute ground spices, add lemongrass, galangal, bay leaves. Then add coconut milk, after boiling add vegetables. Add salt and stock powder. Cook until done

* Mix all ingredients, then cook in a pan until boiling. Lift the kumut on top with a ladle/strainer, so that the water below is not carried. Note: Or you can also mix egg white and opor sauce, then cook in a pan while continuing to stir

* Serve liwet rice with all the side dishes. Oh...so delicious!
